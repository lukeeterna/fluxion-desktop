Italian PMI landing pages (WeGest, FattureInCloud, DrawDent) use 100% "tu" tone, zero technical jargon in headlines, and lead with euro outcomes or fear scenarios rather than feature names.

**Why:** Politecnico MI data (2025): 59% of Italian SMBs report lack of digital competency — jargon creates distance, not trust. Fresha/Booksy pattern confirmed: metric first (89% fewer no-shows), pain second, mechanism third.

**How to apply:**
- Remove tech siglas (AES-256, VAS/Oswestry/NDI, FDI, Art. 9 GDPR) from headlines — move to body notes
- Headline structure: Euro result OR fear scenario → pain in 1 sentence → automated mechanism
- Paragraphs: max 40 words for hero cards (current FLUXION hero paragraphs run 78-85 words)
- Bullets: verb + benefit, never verb + technical description
- Legal/compliance copy: cite the consequence (€20M multa) not the regulation number (Art. 9 GDPR)
- Brand names that ARE known to Italian PMI: Aruba, Fattura24, WhatsApp, SDI — keep these
- Brand names that are NOT known: Groq, Edge-TTS, Node.js, AES-256-GCM — never in primary copy
